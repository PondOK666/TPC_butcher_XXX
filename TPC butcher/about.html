Frequently Asked Questions.

Beef can be divided into various cuts, including steaks (e.g., ribeye, sirloin, T-bone), roasts (e.g., chuck roast, brisket), ground beef (used for burgers and various dishes), and more.

To ensure safety, cook beef to a minimum internal temperature of 145°F (63°C) for steaks and roasts and 160°F (71°C) for ground beef. Use a meat thermometer to check the temperature.

Beef should be stored in the refrigerator at temperatures below 40°F (4°C). It's best to keep it in its original packaging or airtight containers to prevent cross-contamination. Use or freeze beef within a few days of purchase.
